---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the **NotificationService** of the Voxarium Application Layer.

NotificationService coordinates application workflows responsible for delivering notifications generated during Application execution. The service provides a unified mechanism for creating, managing, and distributing notifications while remaining independent of user interface frameworks, messaging systems, and Infrastructure delivery mechanisms.

The service SHALL coordinate notification workflows but SHALL NOT implement notification delivery technologies.

---

# 2. Scope

This specification defines:

* responsibilities;
* supported notification operations;
* notification lifecycle;
* dependencies;
* transactional behavior.

GUI frameworks, operating system notifications, e-mail providers, messaging platforms, push notification services, and Infrastructure implementations are outside the scope of this specification.

---

# 3. Definition

NotificationService is an Application Service responsible for coordinating notifications generated by Application workflows.

Notifications SHALL represent informational messages about Application events and SHALL NOT contain business logic.

---

# 4. Responsibilities

NotificationService SHALL be responsible for:

* creating notifications;
* validating notification requests;
* determining notification priority;
* coordinating notification channels;
* tracking notification state;
* dismissing notifications;
* publishing notification-related Domain Events where applicable.

The service SHALL NOT:

* display notifications directly;
* communicate with operating system notification APIs;
* send e-mail messages;
* implement messaging protocols.

---

# 5. Dependencies

NotificationService MAY depend upon:

* EventBus;
* RepositoryContract;
* UnitOfWork;
* TransactionCoordinator;
* ApplicationValidator;
* ApplicationMapper;
* OperationResult.

The service SHALL depend only upon notification abstraction contracts.

---

# 6. Supported Operations

Typical operations include:

* CreateNotification;
* PublishNotification;
* DismissNotification;
* GetNotification;
* ListNotifications;
* MarkAsRead;
* ClearNotifications;
* GetNotificationStatus.

Additional operations MAY be introduced provided they remain consistent with Application Layer responsibilities.

---

# 7. Notification Lifecycle

A notification SHOULD follow this lifecycle:

1. creation;
2. validation;
3. publication;
4. delivery to configured notification providers;
5. acknowledgement or dismissal;
6. archival or removal according to retention policy.

Notification state transitions SHALL remain deterministic.

---

# 8. Transaction Management

Notifications associated with Domain changes SHALL be published only after successful transaction completion.

Rollback SHALL prevent publication of notifications related to failed Application operations.

Notification persistence, where applicable, SHALL remain transactionally consistent.

---

# 9. Validation

Before publishing a notification, the service SHALL validate:

* notification type;
* message completeness;
* target audience;
* priority;
* application constraints.

Validation failures SHALL prevent publication.

---

# 10. Error Handling

Failures SHALL be represented using OperationResult or standardized Application exceptions.

Infrastructure-specific notification failures SHALL be translated into Application-level failures before leaving the Application Layer.

Notification failures SHALL NOT corrupt Domain state.

---

# 11. Thread Safety

NotificationService SHOULD remain stateless.

Concurrent notification operations SHALL execute independently using isolated execution contexts.

Notification consistency SHALL be preserved through transactional coordination.

---

# 12. Compliance

All notification workflows within Voxarium SHALL be coordinated through NotificationService or an equivalent Application Service conforming to this specification.

Implementations SHALL preserve deterministic behavior, dependency inversion, architectural isolation, transactional consistency, and complete separation between Application orchestration and Infrastructure notification technologies.
